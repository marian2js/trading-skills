# Pattern Framework

Use this framework to review a trade journal for repeatable strengths and mistakes.

## What a journal review should answer

- What is actually repeating?
- Is the pattern about process, environment, or both?
- Is the sample good enough to trust?
- What is the one rule or change worth testing next?

## What to group by

When possible, group trades by:

- setup type
- timeframe
- instrument
- sector or theme
- catalyst context
- adherence quality
- market regime

## Common repeatable patterns

Helpful patterns:

- strongest setups are concentrated in one environment
- best trades share better preparation or cleaner invalidation
- worst trades cluster around event holds, open entries, or oversizing

Risky patterns:

- widening stops after entry
- late chasing
- taking profits too early relative to the plan
- oversizing after recent wins
- forcing trades in poor regimes
- holding through catalysts without explicit reasons

## Sample discipline

Be careful when:

- the sample is very small
- only the most memorable trades are included
- notes are sparse
- different strategies are mixed together without separation

## Good process changes

Good changes are:

- specific
- testable
- narrow

Examples:

- no breakout entries in the first 15 minutes
- no event holds without a written reason
- cut size after two process violations in a week

Bad changes are:

- trade better
- be more disciplined
- trust yourself more

## Boundaries

This framework does not prove a durable edge.

It only helps the user learn from recorded behavior and improve process quality.
