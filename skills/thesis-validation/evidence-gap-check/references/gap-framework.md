# Evidence Gap Framework

Use this framework to decide whether an idea has enough support to move forward or whether research is still missing in the areas that matter most.

## Core question

The point is not "what more could I learn?"

The point is:

- what do I still need to know before this decision deserves action?

## Separate three buckets

- `known`: evidence the user actually has
- `assumed`: beliefs or inferences that still need confirmation
- `missing`: information that matters to the decision but has not yet been checked

## Typical evidence categories

Depending on the idea and timeframe, gaps may exist in:

- catalyst timing
- business fundamentals
- valuation context
- competitive landscape
- management guidance
- price structure and trend
- macro sensitivity
- sector leadership or breadth
- liquidity and tradability
- invalidation conditions

## Blocking vs non-blocking gaps

Blocking gaps:

- the claim is not clear
- the timeframe does not match the evidence
- the user cannot say what would invalidate the idea
- a major catalyst or dependency is unknown
- the key reason the idea should work has not actually been checked

Non-blocking gaps:

- extra context that would improve confidence
- secondary comparative work
- lower-priority background details

## What a good output should do

- reduce false confidence
- prioritize the missing information
- point to one next research step
- keep the user from jumping into construction or execution too early

## Boundaries

This framework does not decide:

- whether the thesis is valid
- whether reward-to-risk is attractive
- whether the order plan is executable
- whether the portfolio can absorb the position

It only identifies whether the evidence base is sufficiently complete for the next step.
