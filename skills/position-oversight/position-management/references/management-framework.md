# Position Management Framework

Use this framework to manage an open position without drifting into improvisation.

## Core question

The question is not only:

- "Am I up or down?"

The real questions are:

- Is the thesis still intact?
- Has the risk profile changed?
- Does a catalyst change the holding decision?
- What is the next clean action?

## Common management actions

- `hold`: thesis and management plan still fit
- `trim`: realize part of the gain or reduce event exposure
- `tighten risk`: reduce giveback or adapt to weakening conditions
- `close`: thesis broken, catalyst risk too high, or discipline deteriorating
- `wait for condition`: no change yet, but only if the condition is explicit

## Typical mistakes

- widening stops after entry without a new thesis
- refusing to trim because of fear of missing out
- tightening stops purely because unrealized PnL feels uncomfortable
- holding through earnings or macro catalysts by default
- treating a temporary bounce as proof the thesis is restored

## Winners versus losers

For winners:

- ask whether the position has already delivered enough to justify different management
- protect against turning a good trade into a round trip
- avoid trimming only because of emotion if the thesis and trend are still strong

For losers:

- check whether the invalidation condition is already near or already hit
- do not widen risk casually
- separate temporary noise from genuine thesis deterioration

## Catalyst-aware management

Upcoming catalysts can change the right action even if price has not moved much yet.

Review:

- earnings dates
- macro releases
- central-bank events
- policy decisions
- company-specific milestones

If the event can materially compress the decision window, management should become more explicit, not less.

## Boundaries

This framework does not decide:

- whether the original entry was good
- how much size should have been taken initially
- whether the portfolio was too concentrated before entry

It only helps manage the position that already exists.
