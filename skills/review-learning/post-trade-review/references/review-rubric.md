# Review Rubric

## Thesis

- Was the original idea clear enough to invalidate?
- Was the catalyst or setup actually relevant?

## Execution

- Did the entry match the plan?
- Was the size appropriate for the setup quality and volatility?

## Adherence

- Was the stop honored?
- Were targets managed according to the plan?
- Were any rules changed mid-trade?

## Mistakes

Classify mistakes without drama:

- process mistake
- emotional override
- poor sizing
- weak thesis
- wrong environment read

## Lessons

The best lesson is specific and repeatable:

- "Reduce size by 25% into high-impact data days."
- "Do not widen stops after entry unless the original plan explicitly allowed it."
